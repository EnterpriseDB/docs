When you upgrade your WarehousePG cluster, you must perform some additional steps in order to bring your PXF configuration up to date.

There are two possible scenarios:

1.  *WarehousePG 5.x to 6.x upgrade or migration*:
    -   If you are using the `gphdfs` external table protocol to access data stored in Hadoop in your WarehousePG 5.x installation, you must first [migrate your gphdfs external tables](gphdfs-pxf-migrate.html) to use PXF instead, since WarehousePG version 6 removes support for the `gphdfs` protocol.
    -   PXF actions are required if you choose a [gpupgrade](https://docs.vmware.com/en/VMware-Greenplum-Upgrade/1.6/greenplum-upgrade/index.html)-based WarehousePG 5.x to 6.x upgrade.
    -   PXF actions are required if you choose to manually [migrate from WarehousePG 5.x to 6.x](migrate_5to6.html).

2.  *WarehousePG 6.x to 6.newer upgrade*: You may be required to perform PXF upgrade actions when you [upgrade from a previous WarehousePG 6.x version](upgrade_pxf_6x.mdx). Note that different steps are outlined based on your PXF installation source: a manually installed `rpm` or `deb` package downloaded from VMware Tanzu Network, or the PXF version included with the WarehousePG Server installer in versions 6.18.x and older. 

If you are upgrading a PXF `rpm` or `deb` installation independent of a WarehousePG upgrade, refer to the [PXF upgrade documentation](https://docs.vmware.com/en/VMware-Greenplum-Platform-Extension-Framework/6.6/greenplum-platform-extension-framework/upgrade_landing.html).
